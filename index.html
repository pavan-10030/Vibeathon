Vibe-A-thon Rules & Guidelines

Eligibility & Teams

Teams can consist of 1-4 members. All participants must be registered. Anyone is welcome, regardless of skill level.

Project Submission

All projects must be new and developed entirely during the hackathon. Submissions should include a working prototype and a short demonstration video.

Intellectual Property

Participants own the intellectual property of their projects. However, by participating, you agree to have your project displayed on the event website and social media.

Code of Conduct

Participants must adhere to the hackathon's Code of Conduct, promoting a safe, inclusive, and respectful environment for all.

Judging Criteria

Projects will be judged on innovation, technical implementation, and real-world impact. Bonus points for using cutting-edge AI technologies.

Prizes

Prizes will be awarded to the top three teams. All participants will receive a certificate of participation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an AI Hackathon?
An AI Hackathon is a competitive event where participants collaborate to build software projects centered around Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning. Teams work intensively over a short period to develop innovative solutions, prototypes, or applications.
Who can participate?
Anyone can participate! The hackathon is open to students, professionals, and enthusiasts of all skill levels. We encourage people from diverse backgrounds, including developers, designers, data scientists, and project managers, to form teams.
Do I need to have a team beforehand?
No, you don't. You can form a team with friends or other participants on-site during the team formation session at the beginning of the event. We will help connect you with others who have similar interests or skills.
What should I bring?
Bring your laptop, charger, and any other necessary hardware you might need for your project. We'll provide the rest, including Wi-Fi, food, drinks, and a comfortable working environment.
Will there be mentors available?
Yes! We will have a team of experienced mentors from the AI and tech industry available throughout the event to provide guidance, technical support, and feedback on your projects.
What are the judging criteria?
Projects will be judged on innovation, technical complexity, potential impact, and the quality of the presentation. A clear use of AI or machine learning is a key factor.
